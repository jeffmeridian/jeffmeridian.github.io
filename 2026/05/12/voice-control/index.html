To navigate efficiently using only Voice Control, you can leverage a mix of structural commands for the browser and text-editing commands for your writing.


Here is the "complete file" of keywords and methods for your specific workflow.

---

## 1. Navigating Websites (Brave / Chrome / Safari)
When on a website, names of buttons aren't always obvious. These commands bypass the need to know the "name" of a link.

* "Show numbers": This is your primary tool. Every link and button gets a small number. Say the number to click it (e.g., "Click 17").
* "Show grid": For clicking things that aren't buttons (like a specific part of a video or a map). Say a number to zoom in, then say it again to click.
* "Scroll down" / "Scroll up": Moves the page by one screen.
* "Scroll to top" / "Scroll to bottom": Immediate jumps.
* "Click [Name]": If a button says "Sign In," just say "Click Sign In."

Tab and Window Management
* "Press Command T": Open new tab.
* "Press Command W": Close current tab.
* "Press Control Tab": Switch to the next tab to the right.
* "Press Control Shift Tab": Switch to the tab to the left.
* "Press Command L": Jump to the address bar to type a new URL.

---

## 2. Opening Applications
You do not need to go to the Dock.
* "Open Brave"
* "Open Notes"
* "Switch to Finder" (Moves focus to an app that is already open).
* "Quit [App Name]"

---

## 3. Creating Your Own Keywords (Custom Shortcuts)
You can create a "Watch Word" for any complex keyboard shortcut.

1. Go to System Settings andgt; Accessibility andgt; Voice Control.
2. Click Commands... and then the [+] button.
3. When I say: Enter your keyword (e.g., "Refresh Page" or "Send Email").
4. While using: Select "Any Application" or just "Brave."
5. Perform: Choose Press Keyboard Shortcut.
6. Press the keys on your keyboard (e.g., Cmd + R or Cmd + Shift + D).
7. Click Done. Now, saying your phrase will trigger that specific shortcut.

---

## 4. Text Editing and Rephrasing
These commands allow you to modify text without ever touching a mouse.

Highlighting and Selecting
* "Select [Word]": Highlights a specific word.
* "Select next two words": Highlights the text immediately following your cursor.
* "Select previous sentence": Highlights the sentence you just finished.
* "Select all": Highlights everything in the text box.

Removing and Replacing
* "Delete that": Deletes the last thing you said or the currently selected text.
* "Delete [Word]": Deletes a specific word anywhere in the paragraph.
* "Delete previous paragraph": Removes the block of text above.
* "Replace [Word] with [New Word]": This is the fastest way to rephrase (e.g., "Replace happy with ecstatic").

Rephrasing / Formatting
* "Correct that": Opens a list of alternative words if the Mac misheard you.
* "Capitalize [Word]": Changes "apple" to "Apple."
* "Lowercase that": Fixes accidental caps.
* "Bold that" / "Underline that": Standard formatting for the selected text.

---

Critical Workflow Tip: "Command Mode"
To avoid the Mac accidentally typing your commands into a search bar, use these two toggles:
1. "Command Mode": The Mac stops typing. It only listens for things like "Open Brave" or "Scroll down." Use this when browsing.
2. "Dictation Mode": The Mac starts typing. Use this only when you have clicked into a text box and are ready to write.
